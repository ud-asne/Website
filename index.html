// Our Mission

Engineering
Excellence,
Built by Students

We are a student-led engineering team at the University of Delaware, dedicated to hands-on innovation in marine robotics. From designing hulls in Rhino3D to writing autonomous navigation code in ROS, every system is conceived, built, and tested by our members.

As participants in the ASNE Powered Electric Propulsion (PEP) competition, we push the boundaries of what a university team can build — and in our first year, we proved it with a Top-10 speed finish.

⚙️
End-to-End Engineering Hull design, propulsion, circuit schematics, autonomous navigation — all in-house.
🎓
Learn by Doing No prior experience required. We mentor students across all engineering disciplines.
🌊
Real Competition We race against top universities on open water at the ASNE PEP national event.

// Recognition & Milestones

Year One Achievements

🏆

Top-10 Speed Finish

As a first-year team, we placed in the Top 10 for speed at the ASNE PEP 2025 competition — competing against established university programs from across the country.

📰

UDaily Feature

Our work was recognized in a UDaily article, highlighting the team's technical achievements and commitment to advancing marine robotics at the University of Delaware.

⚓

US Navy 250th Homecoming

Selected to represent the University of Delaware at the US Navy's 250th Anniversary Homecoming event in November 2025, showcasing our autonomous vessel to naval engineers and officials.

🎤

ME Department Presentation

Presented our full 2025 build to the Mechanical Engineering department, demonstrating our design process, propulsion system, and competition results to faculty and fellow students.

🏗️

Custom Hull — From Scratch

Designed, simulated, and built our 2026 vessel from the ground up using Rhino3D, Orca3D, SolidWorks, and Fusion 360, including full drag analysis and propeller dimensioning.

🤝

Industry Sessions

Hosted technical sessions with Professor Michael DeLorme of Stevens Institute of Technology to build student knowledge in naval architecture and marine propulsion systems.
